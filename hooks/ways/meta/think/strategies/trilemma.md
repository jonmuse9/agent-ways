# Trilemma

## Signature
keywords: balance trade-off competing three objectives tension pick two can't have all sacrifice versus
## Stages

### 1. Problem Reception
Identify the three competing objectives. Name them clearly. Example: speed vs cost vs reliability.

### 2. Threshold Setting
For each objective, define "good enough" — the minimum acceptable level. This is satisficing, not maximizing. You cannot optimize all three; you can satisfy all three.

### 3. Trade-Off Mapping
Build a matrix: improving objective A by N% costs objective B by X% and objective C by Y%. Make the trade-offs explicit and quantified where possible.

### 4. Satisficing Proposal
Propose a solution. Score it against all three thresholds. Does it meet "good enough" on each?

### 5. Equilibrium Check
If any objective is below threshold, adjust. What's the minimum change to bring it above threshold without pushing another below? If impossible, revisit the thresholds — one of them may be unrealistic.

### 6. Solution Refinement
Document the final balance point and the accepted trade-offs. Be explicit about what was sacrificed and why.
